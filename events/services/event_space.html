Event Spaces

Located just a few blocks from the UC Berkeley campus and within driving distance from Oakland and San Francisco International Airports, the picturesque Spanish colonial style Clark Kerr Conference Center is available for conferences, meetings and special functions from mid-August through mid-May.

Our comfortable meeting spaces, audio-visual support, ample parking, business center, and exceptional catering services make Clark Kerr Conference Center the best kept secret in the Bay Area!

History

The California School for the Deaf and Blind was founded in a small facility in San Francisco in 1860, with the State appropriating $10,000 toward building a structure. 130 acres of land adjoining a tract belonging to the College of California (later named the University of California, Berkeley) were purchased in February 1867 for $12,100.

Under Dr. Warring Wilkinson’s leadership, on July 29, 1867 ground was broken for the new school using locally quarried sandstone. The entire structure was completely destroyed by fire in 1875. New construction began in 1877, and the second design of the institution followed the cottage or segregated system of detached structures in an informal English garden design. The plan was considered safer, more economical and allowed for indefinite expansion.

Respected throughout the country as an innovative environment for the teaching of the deaf and the blind, the school’s success is directly attributed to the energy and devotion of Warring Wilkinson, who served as superintendent for over four decades until his retirement in 1909. Dr. Wilkinson reported in 1909 that over 1,100 students had attended the Institution since it opened, and fifteen had become college graduates.

By 1921 a third building plan for the site was necessitated, with the state mandate to separate the two schools. This time in striking contrast to the existing Romanesque revival buildings, a new Spanish Colonial Revival vocabulary of architecture was developed and would continue through the 1940s. By the 1970s all of the original masonry buildings were gone. The lovely Mediterranean-style buildings which replaced them, and now occupy the site, are the finest representation of the development of the Spanish Colonial Revival tradition in California’s public institutions today.

With concerns of safety arising again in the late 60s and early 70s, costs of renovating older buildings, and worries over earthquake fault lines (later proving to be inactive), research eventually led to the schools’ move to a new site in Fremont in 1980.

In 1982 the University acquired 50 acres of the site and began extensive renovations to turn the complex into a new housing facility for 750 University students, with a spacious and convenient conference center. The complex is a registered city, state, and national landmark.

The complex was dedicated to Clark Kerr in November 1986. He became Berkeley’s first Chancellor in 1952 having won respect for his thoughtfulness during the Loyalty Oath controversy. As chancellor he was determined to reinstate an atmosphere of freedom so damaged in the wake of the dispute.

As University housing and conference facilities, the Clark Kerr Campus has hosted Al Gore, Hillary Rodham Clinton, and the inaugural of Chancellor Robert Berdahl.

Meeting Room Rates

These rates apply to functions held Monday – Friday 8am-5pm during mid-August through mid-May. Rental rates may differ for summer conference guests. For summer conference inquiries, please contact meethere@berkeley.edu

Campus Users include departments, campus affiliated organizations, student groups, or other campus units which are recognized by the University.

Non-Campus Users with Campus Sponsorship include non-campus uses who have an established sponsorship with a campus group. Non-campus users with campus sponsorship are required to use campus sponsor for all billing of event expenses. In the event of non-payment by non-campus users with campus sponsorship, the campus sponsor will be responsible for all expenses incurred for the event.

Non-Campus Users are welcome to use the conference center and dining facilities for meeting and special functions; University affiliation is not required.

Facility Use Policy

Reservation & Cancellation
  • A 50% deposit based on the estimated facility rental fee at the time of initial inquiry is required to reserve an event date.
  • If deposit and signed contract are not received 14 days after issuance of the contract, the reservation will be released automatically without notice.
  • Balance of expenses is due on the date of the event.
  • New reservations will not be accepted if there is an overdue balance.
  • Cancellations must be received in writing and confirmed by Catering Sales Manager.
  • Cancellations submitted within 21 days prior to event will be billed for the full amount of the event.
  • Fire safety mandates that maximum capacity for each room must be strictly enforced. Cal Catering reserves the right to change a designated room if the guest count increases or decreases.
Security
  • Sponsors are responsible for providing sufficient staff to ensure that their event runs smoothly at all times.
  • For certain public events, police security is mandatory. All police security must be arranged through the University of California Police Department.
  • Smoking is prohibited in any common area or within 20 feet of all exterior building entrances and exits.
  • Open flames or flammable materials are also prohibited.
  • All events must conclude by 10pm.
Billing & Fees
  • Facility rental fee includes the following services: pre-arranged room set-up, breakdown, trash disposal, and complementary water station.
  • Room set-up changes made after event confirmation may incur an additional fee.
  • Excess trash, debris, or damage done to facilities will incur a cleaning or damages fee based on the degree of severity
  • Should an event run past the agreed upon duration, a minimum of $100.00 per half hour overtime charge may apply.
  • Any damages to equipment and facilities will be assessed and fees including potential replacement costs will be added.
  • Any group in violation of the terms and conditions listed is subject to additional fees and may lose future rental privileges.
Reserve a Room

Contact our sales office at 510-643-4314 to inquire about reserving a meeting space. Please have the following information available: event date, time, number of expected attendees, and whether catering services are required.
